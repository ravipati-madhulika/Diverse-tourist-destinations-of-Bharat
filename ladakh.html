LADAKH



Panaromic hotspring

Panamik is a wonderful village surrounded by high mountain peaks in Ladakh. Ladakh is a Union Territory of India and Panamik village is situated on the Indo Tibetan Border in Ladakh. Panamik is also near the Siachen Glacier camp area. Panamik village is well known for its panamik hot springs. The sulphur rich hot water springs are the main reason for making this village famous and the springs have come to be known as the ‘Panamik Hot Water Springs’. Panamik village is a small yet an interesting place to visit, on your trip to Ladakh.

Weather:7 - 21°C

Best Time to visit:Summer season in the months of June, July and August.

Turtuk

There is much to keep your senses enthralled in Turtuk. Explore the picturesque Turtuk Village and stroll through its narrow lanes, admiring the traditional stone houses adorned with beautiful gardens. Once you&rsquore done seeing the village, head over to the Turtuk Waterfall, having taken a short hike to the small lake beside the waterfalls as you enjoy the scenic beauty of the cascading water amidst the rugged mountain landscape. Monastery of Diskit, the oldest and largest Buddhist monastery in the Nubra Valley, is a great place for seeking solace and an excellent trek. It offers panoramic views of the valley and houses a 32-meter-tall statue of Maitreya Buddha. Another popular spot is the Yagbo Ancient Village. Venture into this ancient village nearby, and witness the remains of a centuries-old settlement, including rock carvings and ruins.

Best Time to visit:April to September
